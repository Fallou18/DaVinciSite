Workshops

Step into a world where artistry and intellect intertwine with Humanistic Da Vinci’s workshops. Each workshop is an odyssey into the heart of human creativity, offering a unique blend of practical artistic skills and enriching intellectual discussions. Our sessions are carefully crafted to not only teach the techniques of great masters but also to provide insights into the philosophical underpinnings that have shaped humanistic thought throughout history. From painting and sculpture to literature and history, our workshops are designed to ignite your passion for learning and create an engaging environment where ideas flourish. Whether you’re honing your artistic talents or seeking deeper understanding of the humanistic legacy, our workshops provide an unparalleled opportunity to grow and connect.

The Art and Philosophy of Baroque»

Workshop Description:

Immerse yourself in the dramatic and expressive world of Baroque art and philosophy. This workshop offers a unique opportunity to explore the intricate relationship between artistic expression and philosophical thought during the Baroque period.

Agenda:
  • 10:00 AM – Introduction to the Baroque Era: An insightful talk exploring the cultural and historical context of the Baroque period.
  • 10:45 AM – Baroque Art Techniques Session: Hands-on session where you’ll learn and practice the bold, dynamic painting techniques characteristic of Baroque art.
  • 12:30 PM – Lunch Break: Enjoy a catered lunch and informal discussions with fellow attendees.
  • 1:30 PM – Philosophy and the Arts: Interactive seminar discussing the philosophical themes and ideas that influenced Baroque artists.
  • 2:45 PM – Collaborative Art Project: Apply your newly learned skills and ideas in a collaborative artwork, symbolizing the fusion of art and philosophy.
  • 3:45 PM – Closing Discussion and Exhibition:Reflect on the day’s experiences and showcase the collaborative artwork. Opportunity for feedback and networking.
    • Who Should Attend:

    This workshop is ideal for artists, art students, philosophers, and anyone interested in the Baroque period. It’s a perfect setting for those looking to deepen their artistic skills and philosophical understanding in a collaborative environment.

    Additional Information:

    Workshop fee: Contact us.
    Pre-registration is required as spaces are limited.
    No prior experience in art or philosophy necessary.
